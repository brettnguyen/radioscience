[Shared Knowledge]
The entire system shares a small set of scientific conventions and constants. Distances in public APIs should be explicit in names, usually *_km for geolocation/BP distances and *_m for ECEF coordinates and propagation grids. Phase is in radians. Wavelength is in meters. Positions are ECEF unless a method explicitly says geodetic. Candidate BP distances and magnetic-field distances both use 100 to 6000 km with 100 km step by default. The key geolocation equation is D(L_mf)=L0-L_mf, where L0 is the distance of the global local minimum of smoothed V(L) for a BP plane defined by magnetic field at L_mf. The normalized BP amplitude variance is V=<A^2>/<A>^2 - 1. The QC thresholds are sigma_phi>0.25 rad, tangent height>150 km, Q>1.2, cos(alpha)>0.1, single-valued zero crossing only, and valid local minimum away from endpoints with L1/L2 at least two 100 km samples. The synthetic phase-screen defaults are spectral index p=1.5 and outer scale l0=10 km. Real-data monthly maps use accepted post-sunset geolocations only, local time 18-24 hr, and 3 degree by 3 degree bins. All modules should use the shared dataclasses in src/core/types.py and configuration in AppConfig rather than duplicating fields or hard-coding parameters.

[Anything UNCLEAR]
The paper leaves several details unresolved. The exact COSMIC-2 HR data schema and whether CDAAC products already provide demodulated/connected GLONASS phase must be verified. The exact phase detrending method used before sigma_phi calculation is unspecified. The operational implementation may have used FFT plane-wave BP or Kirchhoff integral BP; the design defaults to FFT with stationary-correction Option 2 but should support comparison with Option 1/Kirchhoff later. The exact SNR-to-amplitude normalization used for BP is not fully specified. The parsed paper omitted the Kirchhoff diffraction integral equation. The sign convention in the final geolocation vector must be validated against real geometry. The paper is inconsistent about the L1/L2 comparison duration: the abstract says 2 months, while the body says February 19 to March 1, 2021. The local-time reference for post-sunset filtering is not explicitly stated; this design uses geolocation longitude by default.
